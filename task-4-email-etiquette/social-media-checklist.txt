Responsible Social Media Use for College Students

 
Do’s 
•	Curate your digital footprint. Remember that future employers and professors often search for candidates online, so highlight your projects and positive interests. 
•	Audit your privacy settings. Regularly check and update who can see your posts, photos, and personal information across all platforms. 
•	Network professionally. Connect with classmates, professors, alumni, and industry professionals in your field. 
•	Verify information before sharing. Check the credibility of a source before sharing an article or post to avoid spreading misinformation. 
•	Use the 24-hour rule for emotional posts. If you are angry or upset about a grade or a situation, wait a full day before posting about it to ensure you are thinking clearly. 
 
DON’Ts 
 
•	Don't post compromising content. Avoid sharing photos of illegal activities, excessive partying, or anything you wouldn't want a hiring manager to see. 
•	Don't overshare personal security details. Never publicly post your exact location in real-time, your daily class schedule, or your dorm room number. 
•	Don't engage in toxic arguments. Avoid getting dragged into heated, disrespectful debates since screenshots last forever, even if you delete the comment. 
•	Don't let scrolling replace sleeping or studying. Be mindful of your screen time so social media doesn't distract you from your coursework or well-being. 
•	Don't speak negatively about your university or peers. Venting online about specific professors or classmates can quickly backfire and cause unnecessary drama or disciplinary action.
